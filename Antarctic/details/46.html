ASPA 141: Yukidori Valley, Langhovde, Lützow-Holm Bay
 
Proponent: Japan
Location: 69°14'30"S  39°46'00"E
Approx. Area: 4.88 km2
Summary: Yukidori Valley is situated in the middle part of Langhovde, on the east coast of Lützow-Holm Bay, Continental Antarctica. The area is representative of the typical Antarctic fellfield ecosystem. Permanent quadrats for monitoring lichen and moss vegetation have been established in this typical continental ecosystem to investigate long-term environmental change. Considering the increasing pedestrian traffic from the nearby Syowa Station (Japan) the aim is to keep human impacts on the ecosystem to a minimum and protect scientific research.
Designation: Rec XIV-5
Last Review: M 1 (2000)
Next Review: 2013
Annex V format adoption: 2000
Modification / Review summary: Designated SSSI 22 in Rec. XIV-5 (1987). After an extension of the original Management Plan, a revised version was adopted in Measure 1 (2000). The site was re-designated ASPA 141 in Decision 1 (2002). The Management Plan was reviewed and continued without changes at CEP XI (2008).
Primary reason for designation: B - representative examples of major terrestrial, including glacial and aquatic, ecosystems and marine ecosystems;
Main Environmental Domain represented: D - East Antarctic coastal geologic (e.g. Vestfold, Bunger, Wilson hills)
