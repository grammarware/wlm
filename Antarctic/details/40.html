ASPA 135: North-east Bailey Peninsula, Budd Coast, Wilkes Land
 
Proponent: Australia
Location: 66Â°17'S  110Â°33'E
Approx. Area: 0.28 km2
Summary: The Area is located on Bailey Peninsula in the Windmill Islands region of Budd Coast, Wilkes Land, East Antarctica. The site contains contrasting habitats and water bodies, extremely rich lichen and moss communities, and an important stand of liverwort. Proximity to the nearby Casey Station (Australia) minimizes logistical problems for field research and, at the same time, maximizes the potential for disturbance of research in the Area (including studies into the diverse assemblage of vegetation since the early 1980s). It is primarily for the latter reason that the site requires protection.
Designation: Rec XIII-8
Last Review: M 8 (2008)
Next Review: 2013
Annex V format adoption: 2003
Modification / Review summary: Designated SSSI 16 in Rec. XIII-8 (1985). It was re-designated as ASPA 135 in Decision 1 (2002). After two extensions of the original Management Plan, a revised version was adopted in Measure 2 (2003). The current Management Plan was adopted in Measure 8 (2008).
Primary reason for designation: C - areas with important or unusual assemblages of species, including major colonies of breeding native birds or mammals;
Main Environmental Domain represented: D - East Antarctic coastal geologic (e.g. Vestfold, Bunger, Wilson hills)
