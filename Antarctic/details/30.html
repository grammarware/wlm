ASPA 125: Fildes Peninsula, King George Island (25 de Mayo)
 
Proponent: Chile
Location: 62°12'S  58°58'W
Approx. Area: 2.34 km2
Summary: The Fildes Peninsula is the most extensive coastal area free of snow in summer in King George Island (25 de Mayo), with a length of around 7km. It is one of the areas in Antarctica of greatest paleontological interest, owing to the presence of outcrops with fossil remains of a wide range of organisms, including vertebrate and invertebrate ichnites, and abundant flora with impressions of leaves and fronds, trunks, pollen grains and spores that date from the Late Cretaceous to the Eocene period.
Designation: Rec IV-12
Last Review: M 6 (2009)
Next Review: 2014
Annex V format adoption: 2009
Modification / Review summary: Designated SPA 12 in Rec. IV-12 (1966). The SPA was modified in Rec. V-5 (1968), then terminated in Rec. VIII-2 (1975) and re-designated SSSI 5 in Rec. VIII-4 (1975). The site was re-designated ASPA 125 in Decision 1 (2002). After several extensions of the original Management Plan a revised version was adopted in Measure 6 (2009).
Primary reason for designation: F - examples of outstanding geological, glaciological or geomorphological features;
Main Environmental Domain represented: G - Antarctic Peninsula offshore islands (e.g. most of Deception Island)
